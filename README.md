# Vantage Master Portable English Gameplay Project
This is a personal project to translate the text of Vantage Master Portable on the PlayStation Portable from Japanese to English.

## About This Game; What is Vantage Master?
Vantage Master Portable is the 2008 tactics game by Nihon Falcom.
The series started back in 1997 on the PC and even got an English version (even if it was quite rough).
After that, the series didn't get many games aside from VM Japan on the PlayStation 2 (also Japan-only) and this game, Vantage Master Portable.
This was the final entry in the series, but the characters have shown up in some of Falcom's later games like some of the Trails of Cold Steel games,
however, those games were a deck-building minigame, rather than the original tactics gameplay.
While every entry had its original characters, every game included some bonus characters that were from some of Falcom's other series (Brandish, Ys, etc.)
Portable's main inclusion is characters from their Legenf of Heroes: Trails in the Sky trilogy, providing 10 characters to play as.

## General Gameplay
Battles in Vantage Master are always between two Masters: users able to summon monsters called Natials.
The goal of each battle is to attack the opposing Master until their HP reaches zero.
To do this, you can summon Natials to help you fight, each of them being one of four elements: Earth, Water, Fire, Wind
Every element is weak to another and resists one of the others. Masters have no element.
In addition to summoning, Masters can also use spells to change the water level, attack from a distance, heal their Natials slightly, or even cure status effects.
But in order to summon or cast spells, Masters need to use MP, the amount is based on the kind of Natial summoned or spell used.
Natial also have a maintenance cost to keep them on the field, and some even have their own spells they can use.
Masters are able to increase their MP recovery rate by capturing Crystal tiles spread out across the various maps, which also give a defense and HP regen to units standing on them.
To capture a Crystal tile, a unit must end their turn on one to claim control of it. That team will keep control until the opposing side captures the tile.

## About This Patch
This patch focuses on all text in menus, gameplay, textures, and so on.
Dialogue for cutscenes is unaltered, as I do not trust my skills to write coherent dialogue (especially when translating it as well).
Not to mention the dialogue is in the annoying to edit ._as file format.
The goal of this project was to get the game into a state where a player could beat the entire game without needing to know Japanese.
In-depth explanations for rules and gameplay mechanics are available inside the game from the options menu or by opening the menu during gameplay.

## Patching Instructions
Use a program that can use .xdelta files like xdeltaUI to apply the patch to the original Japan ISO.

## Known Bugs/Issues
Due to the limited room in the game's files and having no luck moving the position of some text, there are a few instances of a term being abbreviated.
This is mostly regarding the Unit Details screen, as there was not enough room for terms like Walk1, Swim2, Earth, Water, or Heaven.
Because of this, the following abbreviations were used for the Unit Details and in the bottom-right tooltip:
 - Walk1 -> W1
 - Walk2 -> W2
 - Swim1 -> S1
 - Swim2 -> S2
 - Earth -> Ert
 - Water -> Wtr
 - Fire -> Fir
 - Heaven -> Hvn
